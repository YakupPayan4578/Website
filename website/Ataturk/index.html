The Founder of Modern Turkey

The Life of Mustafa Kemal Atatürk

Educational Life

1886-1893

Started his primary education at Hafız Mehmet Efendi's neighborhood school, later attended Şemsi Efendi School. His teacher, Şemsi Efendi, was the one who gave him the name "Kemal" (meaning perfection) due to his exceptional abilities.

1893-1895

Enrolled in the Salonica Military Middle School (Selanik Askeri Rüştiyesi). It was during this time that his mathematics teacher added 'Kemal' to his name.

1896-1899

Attended Monastir Military High School (Manastır Askeri İdadisi) where he excelled in literature and began writing poems.

1899-1902

Enrolled in the Ottoman Military Academy (Mekteb-i Harbiye-i Şahane) in Istanbul, graduating as a lieutenant in 1902.

Military Career

1905-1907

Served in Damascus as a staff captain. Founded the "Vatan ve Hürriyet" (Homeland and Liberty) secret revolutionary society.

1911-1912

Fought against Italian forces in the Tripolitanian War in Libya, achieving significant military success.

1915

Led Turkish forces in Gallipoli Campaign, successfully defending against Allied powers. This victory made him a national hero and earned him the title of Pasha (General).

1916-1918

Served as commander on various fronts during World War I, including the Caucasus and Palestine fronts.

Presidential Years

1920-1923

Led the Turkish War of Independence and served as the head of the Grand National Assembly. Abolished the Ottoman Sultanate in 1922.

1923

Established the Republic of Turkey and became its first president on October 29, 1923.

1924-1938

Implemented major reforms including:

  • Adoption of the Latin alphabet (1928)
  • Women's suffrage and equal rights (1934)
  • Secular education system
  • Modern civil code
  • Industrial and economic development
